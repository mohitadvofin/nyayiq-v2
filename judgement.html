◆ DELHI HIGH COURT · W.P.(C) 4872/2025

Rajdhani Crafts Industries v. Commissioner CGST

17 APRIL 2026 BENCH: JJ. SINGH & PATNAIK COUNSEL: ADV. SUNIL AGGARWAL GST § 74 ASSESSEE FAVOURED

AI Summary

◆ VERIFIED
◆ Issue
Whether Section 74 of the CGST Act can be invoked where the assessee has duly filed GSTR-3B and GSTR-1 returns disclosing all outward supplies, but has made a computational error in tax payment.
◆ Facts
The petitioner filed all returns for FY 2021-22. A subsequent audit revealed short payment due to a misclassification of rate. The department issued a show-cause notice under Section 74 alleging suppression and invoking extended period of five years.
◆ Held
The Court held that mere non-payment — where all facts were disclosed in returns — does not amount to "suppression of facts" under Section 74. Invocation of extended period was bad in law. Demand order quashed with liberty to department to proceed under Section 73 within limitation if advised.
◆ Ratio
"Suppression" under Section 74 CGST Act requires a positive act of concealment coupled with intent to evade tax. Mere computational error in returns that were fully disclosed cannot attract the extended period of limitation.
◆ Practical implication
Practitioners can challenge Section 74 invocations where the underlying facts were disclosed. The judgement reinforces decades of pre-GST Supreme Court jurisprudence (Pushpam Pharmaceuticals, Continental Foundation) into the GST regime.

[Order of the Court]

Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and the Revenue. The petitioner challenges the demand order dated 12.03.2026 passed by the Respondent No. 2 under Section 74 of the CGST Act, raising demand of ₹42,18,000 along with interest and penalty…

The petitioner submits that all outward supplies were duly reflected in GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B. The alleged short payment arose from misclassification of certain handloom goods under the 5% slab where, according to the department, they ought to have been classified under 12%…

The Court has considered the submissions. The legislative intent of Section 74 is clear from its language — the extended period can be invoked only where tax has not been paid "by reason of fraud, wilful misstatement, or suppression of facts."
